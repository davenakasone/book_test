# Launch plan — 8 weeks, all channels interlocked

**Premise (read before the calendar):**

- **The email list already exists.** Per [PLATFORM.md](PLATFORM.md), you
  start the list 6–12 months out. This 8-week grid is the **marketing
  sprint**, not list day-zero. If you're starting the list *at* week −8,
  you're running the compressed cold-start variant — do it, but expect
  lower launch numbers (the list hasn't had time to warm).
- **This grid shows every channel populated** so the friend can see each
  one done. PLATFORM.md's actual rule is **one video + one social feed** —
  a real solo author on a 5 hr/week budget **deletes one social column
  before week −8** (keep IG *or* LinkedIn, not both). The five-channel
  version below is the demo, not the prescription.
- Effort budget: one person, ~5 hrs/week on platform. The rest goes to the
  *next* book — backlist compounds, marketing decays.

## Standing rules

- **Reviews are the launch's real output.** That's why the ARC track
  exists: reviews should be live on **day 0**, not week +2. Recruit ARC
  readers from the list (the checksum-challenge responders are the natural
  pool). **Amazon caveat:** you may not incentivize reviews or have family
  post them, and reviewers need a purchase history — ARC readers leave
  *honest* reviews, ideally noting they got an advance copy.
- **Broadcast suppression:** dispatch #1 (Giza) overlaps welcome-email 1's
  Giza excerpt, and the week-0 launch email *is* welcome-email 4 — so
  exclude subscribers currently inside the welcome automation from these
  broadcasts, or they get the same email twice in days. Every provider
  supports this segment.
- **Batch production:** all 8 weeks of IG and LinkedIn are written *before*
  week −8 (they're in the playbooks). Only email mailbags and comment
  replies are live-fire.
- **Kill criteria:** any channel with <1 hr/week available or zero traction
  by week +4 gets frozen, not limped. The email list is never frozen.
- **Real numbers, bluntly:** a first satirical/niche book with a cold start
  sells **double digits** in week 1 and that is *normal*. The platform's
  value is measured at book #2 and #3.
