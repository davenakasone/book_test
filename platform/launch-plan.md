# Launch plan — 8 weeks, all channels interlocked

Premise: list-first. Every channel's job is signups until launch week,
sales for one week, then back to signups. Effort budget assumes one
person, ~5 hrs/week on platform (the rest goes to the *next* book, which
is the actual growth strategy — backlist compounds, marketing decays).

## Standing rules

- Reviews are the launch's real output: the +1 week "ask" email matters
  more than launch-day volume. Amazon's algorithm counts reviews;
  everything else is vanity.
- Batch production: all 8 weeks of IG and LinkedIn are written *before*
  week −8 (they're in the playbooks). Only email mailbags and comment
  replies are live-fire.
- Kill criteria (write them down now, thank yourself later): any channel
  with <1 hr/week available or zero traction by week +4 gets frozen, not
  limped. The email list is never frozen.
- The friend's real numbers to expect, bluntly: a first satirical/niche
  book with a cold start sells double digits in week 1 and that is
  *normal*. The platform's value is measured at book #2 and #3.
