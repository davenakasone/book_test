# BUSINESS — what to actually expect, in an investor's frame

Blunt version for someone who finances things and thinks in after-tax
expected value. Everything here is web-sourced (2026-07) and adversarially
fact-checked; drift-prone numbers are flagged. If you read one section,
read the earn-out math.

> **The one-line thesis:** a single indie book is a **negative-EV** bet.
> The median first self-published book earns **under $100/month**. The
> entire investment case rests on the *pipeline* — making book 2, 3, 4…
> nearly free to produce — plus the author actually shipping a backlist.
> If the friend will only ever write one book, the honest expected value
> is a loss. Say so up front.

## The sales reality (kill the fantasy number first)

- **Median first indie book: <$100/month.** Written Word Media's 2025
  Indie Author Survey (n=1,346): ~80% of authors with 1–3 books earn under
  $100/mo; 44% of *all* indie authors do. A debut realistically does a few
  hundred to ~$1,000 in year one — and survey respondents are already
  published, so this *understates* how many first books sell near zero.
- **The $12,755 number is a trap.** The widely-quoted ALLi/CREATe median
  ($12,755, 2022) is a **survivor** figure: authors who'd published ≥1 book
  *and* spent ≥50% of their working time on it. It is the pro-tier number,
  not a debut. Any pitch that grabs it is lying to you.
- **Winner-take-all skew.** Top 1% of indie authors take ~31% of all
  revenue (ALLi/CREATe). The *mean* is not a planning number for one book;
  plan against the *median*, which is near zero.

## Unit economics (what a copy actually nets)

| Product | List | Channel | Net to author |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ebook | $4.99 | KDP 70% (≈2 MB, −$0.15/MB delivery) | **~$3.28** |
| Ebook | $4.99 | KDP 35% (no delivery fee) | ~$1.75 |
| Paperback 6×9 B&W ~90pp | $14.99 | KDP amazon.com (60% − print) | **~$6.69** |
| Paperback 6×9 B&W ~150pp | $14.99 | KDP amazon.com | ~$6.19 |
| Paperback | $14.99 | KDP Expanded Distribution (40% − print) | ~$3.20 |

Two rules fall out: **push the ebook and the Amazon channel** (a bookstore/
library sale via Expanded Distribution nets roughly *half* an Amazon sale),
and **stay inside the $2.99–$9.99 ebook band** for the 70% rate. KDP print
royalty is 60% of list at **≥$9.99** but only **50% at ≤$9.98** — another
reason the print list sits at $14.99, not lower. *(KDP US B&W print cost:
24–110pp = $2.30 flat; 110–828pp = $1.00 + $0.012/page. Verify on KDP's
calculator.)*

## The earn-out math (the number David actually wants)

Cash to recover per title = production spend ÷ per-unit net.

- Production spend, cheap-but-real: **~$2,500–$4,000** (cover + copyedit +
  proofread; see costs below).
- At **~$3.28/ebook**, that's **~750–1,200 ebook sales just to break even
  on cash** — before a cent of profit, before tax.
- The median debut sells **nowhere near that.** The launch plan's own
  honest line ("double digits in week 1 is normal") is the same truth from
  the other side.

So: **title 1 almost certainly loses money.** The bet only works if:

1. Production cost for titles 2+ approaches **~$0** (this repo's whole
   point — the friend reuses the pipeline, pays only for a cover and a
   proofread), **and**
2. The friend actually ships a **backlist.**

## Why backlist is the whole thesis

Catalog size is the single strongest predictor of indie income (Written
Word Media 2025):

| Books published | Median income |
|---|---|
| 1–3 | <$100/month (≈80% of this cohort) |
| ~5 | low hundreds/month |
| ~25–30 | ~$3,000/month median; 40%+ over $5,000 |

Mechanism: each new release gets ~30 days of Amazon new-release boost that
pulls readers into the *entire* backlist. Economics are brutal at book 1,
start working around book **4–5**, and compete at **20–30** titles. **You
are not financing a book. You are financing a book *factory* — and only if
the factory runs.**

## Costs (the cheap-end stack for a book that won't earn out)

Spend like title 1 is a loss leader, because it is.

| Item | Market range | Recommendation for book 1 |
|---|---|---|
| **Cover** | ~$625–$1,250 (Reedsy median ~$880) | **The one spend worth making** — covers drive conversion. ~$700–900, or DIY (Inkscape/GIMP). |
| **Copyedit** | 2.0–3.0¢/word (EFA fiction) | Yes. ~25k-word humor book ≈ $500–750. |
| **Proofread** | 1.2–2.0¢/word | Yes. ~$300–500. |
| **Developmental edit** | 3.0–4.0¢/word | **Skip** unless the manuscript needs structural help. |
| **ISBN** | free (KDP/Ingram) or $125/1, $295/10 (Bowker) | See ISBN note in PUBLISHING.md — buy one owned ISBN *if* going KDP+Ingram wide. |
| **Audiobook** | $1,500–$2,800 (ACX pay) or Royalty Share ($0 upfront, 50/50, 7 yrs) | **Defer** to backlist era, or take Royalty Share. |

EFA per-word rates are for *fiction*; a 60k-word novel's dev+copy+proof
stack runs ~$3,700–$5,400. Our satire is short, which helps.

## What actually moves sales (and what's myth)

- **The "50 reviews unlocks the algorithm" claim is a myth** — it came from
  promo services' own eligibility thresholds, not Amazon. BSR is driven by
  *sales*. What reviews do is lift *conversion*: the first **5–20** reviews
  matter most (one cited figure: +270% purchase likelihood at 5 vs 0).
- **Email list is the most reliable income lever.** Authors *with* a list
  report ~$300/mo median vs ~$100 without. Build it cheaply via newsletter
  swaps / group promos (BookFunnel, StoryOrigin) — cheaper and more durable
  than ads for a debut.
- **Amazon Ads:** break-even ACOS = your margin (~66% at $4.99/70%). Plan
  to *lose* money for the first 60 days (learning phase runs 80–100% ACOS).
  Ads amplify a book that already converts; they don't rescue a weak one.
- **BookBub Featured Deal:** $379–$1,100+ per slot, hard to get accepted,
  rarely pays back on a single title at ~$0.35/sale. A backlist list-
  building play, not a book-1 profit center.
- **ARC/review services** (verify current pricing): NetGalley co-op ~$150
  (1-week featured) to ~$575 (6-mo listing); Kirkus Indie $425 (7–9 wk) /
  $575 (expedited); BookSirens/Booksprout are cheaper ARC-distribution.
  Reviews from ARC readers must be *honest* and disclose the advance copy —
  Amazon bans incentivized reviews.

## Comps & expectations (for the deck)

- **Upside proof it *can* go mass-market:** The Onion's *Our Dumb Century*
  (#1 debut, Thurber Prize) and xkcd's *What If?* (million-copy NYT
  bestseller) — both fake-authoritative-format works, exactly this book's
  lane. This is the ceiling, not the base case.
- **Base case:** a niche category #1 (Humor › Parody) selling modestly.
- **Do NOT model on viral parodies** like *Goodnight Dune* — that's press
  buzz, not documented sales, and carries real trademark/IP risk. A press
  hit is not a revenue model.

## Tax & structure (not tax advice — confirm with a pro)

Directly relevant since the LLC already exists and you think after-tax:

- **Operate as a business (Schedule C) from day one.** As of the July 4,
  2025 OBBBA, **hobby expenses are permanently nondeductible** — if the IRS
  deems the writing a hobby (IRC §183), the income is taxed **gross** and
  your $2,500–$4,000 of production spend buys *no* deduction, which makes
  the break-even worse. The §183 safe harbor: profit in ≥3 of 5 years; keep
  businesslike records.
- **A single-member LLC gives an imprint name and liability separation but
  no tax edge** — it's disregarded and still files Schedule C. Optional for
  book 1.
- **Sales tax:** Amazon/KDP and IngramSpark remit as marketplace
  facilitators, so you don't handle it there. **Direct/event sales** (your
  own site, a table at a con) can trigger *your* state's collection duty —
  or use a merchant-of-record (Gumroad/Payhip) that remits for you.

## The verdict, in your frame

This is a **low-capital, high-optionality** bet — but only scoped as a
*repeatable pipeline*, not a single-title swing. Risk is **~$3–4K per
title** with median return near zero on title 1, versus the ~$100K drone
moonshot you walked away from. The math only clears if production cost
approaches zero for titles 2+ *and* the friend ships a backlist. If the
friend writes exactly one book, the honest EV is a **loss** — fund it as a
hobby you're subsidizing, not an investment. If the friend will write
five, the pipeline is the cheapest book factory money can buy.
